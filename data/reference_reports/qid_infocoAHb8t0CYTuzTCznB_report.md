# AI/ML‑Enabled Radiology Devices First Authorized by FDA (Aug 1, 2023 – Mar 31, 2025)

## Scope and Method
- Source of truth: FDA’s AI/ML‑Enabled Medical Devices list filtered to Radiology and decision dates in range, then each candidate was validated against the FDA 510(k) database (or De Novo, if applicable) to confirm the decision date and that it represents the device’s initial U.S. authorization (not a supplement or later modification). Product codes, device names, and predicates were used to establish lineage [1].
- Inclusion: Devices the FDA tags under Radiology (AI software and imaging device features across CT, MR, X‑ray, ultrasound, mammography, nuclear medicine, interventional radiology). “First cleared” means initial market authorization (initial 510(k) or De Novo), not subsequent changes.
- Deduplication: If the same device line appeared multiple times, only the earliest authorization within the window is retained; if the earliest authorization predates the window, the device is excluded.

Note: The table below includes all entries validated from the provided research for Jan–Mar 2025. Earlier devices in the 2023–2024 portion of the window were not captured in the supplied materials and can be added on request using the same method.

## Verification and Lineage Notes (initial authorization checks)
- Ortho AI (K241696): QIH Automated Radiological Image Processing Software; no earlier Ortho AI 510(k) located; cites KOALA K192109 as predicate; appears to be initial authorization [2].
- JLK‑ICH (K243363): QAS triage/notification; no prior JLK‑ICH authorization found; cites Viz ICH K193658; first authorization for this device name [3].
- Clarify DL (K241350): GE NM/SPECT deep‑learning reconstruction feature; no prior “Clarify DL” 510(k) noted; predicates Xeleris V (K221680) and StarGuide (K210173) [4].
- Prenuvo Body Composition (K242825): New MR‑based body composition SaMD; first authorization under this device name [5].
- TechCare Trauma (K242171): Multi‑anatomy fracture CADe for X‑ray; first U.S. authorization for this Milvue device; predicate BoneView [6].
- Rapid Neuro3D (K243350): New module for Rapid platform; first authorization under “Rapid Neuro3D” [7].
- TeraRecon Aorta.CT (K243158): New, containerized aortic segmentation SaMD; first authorization under this specific device name [8].
- Bunkerhill AVC (K243229): CT‑based AVC software; first authorization under “Bunkerhill AVC” [9].
- aiCockpit AI Viewer (K241546): Viewer that displays AI outputs; first authorization under this device name [10].
- ChestView US (K241620): Gleamer chest X‑ray analyzer; first U.S. authorization for “ChestView US” (distinct from prior BoneView lineage) [11].
- TribusConnect (K242166): Cloud viewer/planning with ML‑based segmentation; first authorization under this device name [12].

## Observations and Trends (Q1 2025 sample)
- Modalities: Broad coverage, spanning CT analytics (AVC, aortic segmentation), X‑ray CADe, NM/SPECT reconstruction, CTA vascular analysis, and MR body composition.
- Product codes: Frequent use of QIH (automated radiological image processing), LLZ (image processing system), MYN (medical image analyzer), and QAS (acute triage/notification), consistent with AI/ML SaMD in radiology [2–12].
- Vendor mix: Blend of established imaging OEMs (GE HealthCare, TeraRecon/ConcertAI) and AI‑first startups (Gleamer, Milvue, BunkerHill, TribusMed).

## Residual Ambiguities and Notes
- FDA AI/ML list presentation: The AI/ML list is a single, continuously updated page, not per‑device pages. The table above links to the list homepage and the device‑specific 510(k) page for definitive details [1–12].
- Naming and lineage: Where vendors have multiple related submissions, lineage was determined by device name, product code, and predicate history in the 510(k) summaries. If the earliest authorization for a device line predated the window, the device was excluded.
