# Top 12 Gaming Monitors You Can Buy in 2025, Ranked (as of Sep 21, 2025)

## How the ranking works
- Scope: standalone computer gaming monitors purchasable new during 2025 (US market and USD pricing unless noted).
- Criteria (equal weighted):
  1) Maximum native refresh rate (Hz) — higher is better
  2) Manufacturer-stated response time (ms) — lower is better. Prefer GtG; if only MPRT is given, use MPRT and label it.
  3) Price per inch (USD per diagonal inch) — lower is better
- Normalization across the 12-candidate set:
  - Refresh normalized = value / max(value)
  - Response-time normalized = min(value) / value
  - Price-per-inch normalized = min(value) / value
  - Overall score = mean of the three normalized values
- Tie-breakers (in order): higher refresh rate, then lower response time, then lower price per inch, then newer release date, then alphabetical by model.
- Price capture window: Sep 21, 2025, approximately 4:50–5:10 PM US Eastern. Listings are new (not refurbished/open-box).

## What stands out and why
- Pure speed leaders: ASUS PG27AQDP (480 Hz native) is unmatched on refresh and ties the OLED class-leading 0.03 ms GtG, easily topping this ranking despite a high $/in [1]. The 360 Hz OLEDs from Samsung, Alienware, GIGABYTE, and MSI cluster closely due to identical response-time claims and similar $/in [2][3][4][5][6][7][8].
- Best value by price-per-inch: LG 32GN600‑B sets the floor for $/in in this set ($5.97/in) and therefore scores well on the cost dimension, though its 5 ms GtG response time drags its response normalized score down [9][10].
- Value ultrawide under $230: Sceptre’s C345B‑QUT168 offers 34" UWQHD 165 Hz at ~$220 for just $6.47/in; it posts a strong price-normalized score, with the expected limitations of a VA panel and MPRT metric [11][12].
- “Spec clarity” note on response metrics: Where both GtG and MPRT are published, the GtG value is used (e.g., Sceptre E325B‑QPN168 lists 1 ms GtG and 1 ms MBR; 1 ms GtG applied). Where the maker only lists MPRT, that is used and labeled (AOC, GIGABYTE value lines).

## Method notes, assumptions, and reproducibility
- Only native max refresh counts (e.g., M32QC’s 170 Hz OC ignored) [17].
- Response-time metric type is recorded explicitly; makers do not measure GtG and MPRT the same way. Normalization strictly followed the brief (lowest stated value in this set is 0.03 ms) [1][2][4][6][8][19].
- Prices are taken from an official store or major US retailer and used directly for price-per-inch (no currency conversion needed). Availability is evidenced by an “Add to Cart” or in-stock indicator at the cited page/time window [1][3][5][7][8][10][12][14][16][18][20][22].
- To reproduce: recompute $/in from the cited prices and sizes, apply the three normalizations against the max/min noted above, and average.
